Research directions

Imaging technology decodes wave-matter interactions and plays a critical role in scientific discoveries. In turn, these discoveries also motivated new scientific goals that require imaging in extreme conditions, such as extreme time scales and extreme resolutions. Our research focuses on computational imaging for science, which leverages the innovative co-design of hardware and algorithms to develop next-generation scientific imaigng instrument. Our work emphasizes both solid theoretical foundations and real impacts in scientific missions. Current lines of investigation include:

Neural PDE - Understanding Imaging Physics

Partial Differential Equations (PDEs) are essential tools for modeling complex imaging physics, which play critical roles in accurately reconstructing images from noisy data in scientific imaging. Neural PDE solvers have recently transformed PDE-based physical modeling, offering both powerful approximation capabilities and enhanced computational efficiency. Our research explores neural PDE methods to tackle unresolved scientific imaging challenges, such as medical ultrasound and 3D optical diffraction tomography. Key areas of focus include:

· Neural PDE based physical modeling

· Deep generative priors

· 3D neural representations

Generative AI - Modeling Image Priors

Generative AI offers powerful tools for modeling complex image distributions using deep neural networks. Leveraging generative AI, we can define powerful data-driven image priors that surpass traditional hand-crafted priors (e.g., sparsity, total variation), achieving better computational image reconstruction qualities. Related research topics include:

·  Deep generative models (e.g., diffusion models, normalizing flows)

·  Neural representations (e.g., neural fields, 3DGS) for imaging

·  (Multi-modal LLMs for imaging)

Adaptive Optics - Automating Imaging Systems

Adaptive optics (AO) is a control system that removes the wavefront aberrations in optical systems. It is very important in microscopy for imaging thick biological samples and in telescopy for imaging through atmospheric turbulence. Topics include:

·  Astronomical adaptive optics (e.g., coronagraphic imaging instrument)

·  Microscopic adaptive optics

·  Machine learning-based adaptive optics
