23310067: Inverse Problems and Computational Imaging (24 Fall, 23 Fall)

TA: Weimin Bai, Enze Ye

Inverse problems are widely applied in many fields of science and engineering, requiring the inference of the state of an observed target from a set of noisy measurement data. Various imaging systems, such as medical ultrasound, X-ray computed tomography, and super-resolution microscopy, are among the primary application scenarios for solving inverse problems. This course aims to introduce the theory of inverse problems and the methods for solving imaging issues, cultivating students' abilities in modeling and solving computational imaging problems in research. The course content is divided into four modules, covering optimization methods and regularization, probabilistic inference, deep learning, and classical imaging applications. Additionally, the course will include four major assignments to develop students' skills in algorithm design and programming for solving inverse problems.

23310072: Adaptive Optics：Principles & Applications（24 Spring）

TA: Jinxin Liu

Adaptive optics technology is an important means of correcting optical aberrations in imaging systems. Adaptive optics originated from ground-based astronomical telescopes, used to compensate for high-speed phase fluctuations caused by atmospheric turbulence, directly leading to significant scientific achievements worthy of a Nobel Prize, such as the discovery of the supermassive black hole. In recent years, adaptive optics technology has gradually found widespread applications in the biomedical field, particularly achieving a series of important breakthroughs in ophthalmic imaging and microscopic imaging. This course aims to introduce the principles and applications of adaptive optics, combining classroom instruction with literature review to cultivate students' ability to apply this technology to solve practical research problems.
